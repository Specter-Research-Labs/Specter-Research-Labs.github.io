We are an independent research group, interested in the overall idea shown by Michael Levin (Levin, 2022) that certain types of patterns are real and exist in a non-spatiotemporal sense, and physical systems are interfaces, or pointers, that let those patterns be expressed.

Active Projects
Morphogenetic Signatures in Sorting Algorithms
status: active-writing
last-activity:2026-03-07

Building on Zang and Levin's sorting-as-morphogenesis work, we continue their study of emergent behaviors in sorting algorithms and find mechanistic interpretations for clustering from temporal synchronization as well as generalization to their findings of delayed gratification. Finally, we come up with a way to measure algorithm-specific robustness-to-"lesions" patterns.

Perturbation-Stable Proof Structures
status:active
scope:expansion

We are interested in proof search as an adaptive dynamical system: do different perturbations lead to the same underlying proof structure, or to qualitatively different proofs? Our experiments run controlled interventions on MCTS theorem proving, logging search trajectories and proof graphs to quantify structural similarity, rerouting, and attractor stability.

Distributed Flow-Lenia Atlas
status:active

Mapping the space of Flow Lenia organisms through parameter sweeps and perturbation experiments on Apple Silicon. Volunteer workers contribute GPU time to expand the shared catalog.

Material Memory in Physical Collectives
status:concept
last-activity:2026-03-07

We are testing whether memory-like behavior can emerge in decentralized physical systems when each unit updates local material properties from interaction history. In this campaign, we compare memory-on runs against blind, no-memory, and inertial controls across imprint, hysteresis, and damage-relearning scenarios. We are exploring whether path dependence and blind-relative search efficiency gains can appear without centralized state.
Read more: Jolt Material Memory Specs.
